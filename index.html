Document No. SPO-MDG-HRM-0020-E · Rev. 00 · Effective 9 July 2026

1. Introduction

1.1 The purpose of this policy is to outline the principles and procedures for reimbursing employees for business-related expenses incurred during the performance of their duties.

1.2 This ensures a standardised and transparent approach to cost recovery across the Master Drilling Group.

1.3 The policy is applicable to all employees within the Group and aims to support operational efficiency, financial control, and compliance with audit and tax requirements.

2. Definitions

2.1 Disbursement: Reimbursement of a cost incurred by an employee for valid business purposes.

2.2 Employee: Any person employed by the Master Drilling Group, including permanent, fixed term, and temporary staff.

2.3 Business-Related Expense: An expense incurred while fulfilling work-related responsibilities, such as travel, accommodation, or approved purchases.

2.4 Supporting Documentation: Evidence substantiating the expense, such as receipts, tax invoices, or proof of payment.

2.5 Pre-Approval: Authorisation obtained from a line manager or relevant authority before incurring the expense.

2.6 Expense Claim Form: The standardised company form used to request reimbursement of business-related expenses.

3. References

3.1 FRM-MDS-FIN-0002-E_Disbursement Form.

4. Acronyms

4.1 HR: Human Resources

5. Company Disbursement Policy

5.1 Employees may claim reimbursement for actual expenses incurred in the execution of their official duties, provided the expenses are:

5.1.1 Necessary and directly related to business operations.

5.1.2 Pre-approved by a line manager or relevant authority.

5.1.3 Accompanied by complete supporting documentation.

5.2 All disbursement claims must be supported by original receipts, tax invoices, or proof of payment. Claims without valid documentation will not be processed.

5.3 All anticipated expenses must be pre-approved. This can be done through email confirmation or completion of an internal approval request, subject to the company's Delegation of Authority.

5.4 Employees must submit disbursement claims within 90 calendar days of the expense being incurred. Late submissions may be declined unless exceptional circumstances are justified and approved by a senior manager.

5.5 Submission and Approval Process.

5.5.1 To Request Reimbursement:

a) Complete the official Disbursement Form.

b) Attach all required supporting documentation.

c) Obtain the necessary approvals.

d) Submit the full claim package to the Payroll Department.

5.6 Payment Timeline.

5.6.1 Claims submitted on or before the 15th of the month will be paid at the end of the same month.

5.6.2 Claims submitted after the 15th of the month will be paid at the end of the following month.

5.6.3 Failure to comply with this policy may result in the rejection or delay of reimbursement claims.

5.6.4 Repeated violations may be referred to Human Resources for further action.
